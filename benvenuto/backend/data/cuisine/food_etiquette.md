# Italian food etiquette and timing

- Cappuccino is a morning drink; ordering one after a meal (or after ~11am) marks you
  as a tourist. After lunch/dinner Italians take an espresso.
- A "caffè" means an espresso, not a large American coffee.
- Bread is for the meal, not usually dipped in olive oil before it.
- Pasta is a first course (primo), not a side dish to meat.
- Coperto is a small cover charge per person — normal, not a scam. Tipping is modest
  or rounded up, not the 15-20% expected in the US.
- Aperitivo (early evening drinks with snacks) is a social institution; cheap and great
  for meeting people.
