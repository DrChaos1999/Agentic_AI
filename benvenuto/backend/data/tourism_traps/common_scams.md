# Common tourist traps in Italy and how to avoid them

- Restaurants near major monuments with photo menus and "tourist menu" boards often
  overcharge for low quality. Walk a few streets away and check that menus list prices
  and a coperto.
- "Free" bracelet/rose: someone ties a bracelet or hands you a rose, then demands money.
  Don't accept anything placed in your hand near tourist sites.
- Fake petitions: a clipboard "petition" is used to distract while an accomplice picks
  pockets. Keep moving.
- Taxis: insist on the meter (tassametro). In Rome there are fixed fares from Fiumicino
  and Ciampino airports to the centre — know the official flat rate before getting in.
- Train stations: ignore "helpers" at ticket machines who expect a tip; buy from
  official machines or counters.
- Venice: water-taxi quotes can be very high; agree the price first, prefer the public
  vaporetto for budget travel.
