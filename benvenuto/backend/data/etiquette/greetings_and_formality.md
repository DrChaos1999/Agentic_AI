# Greetings and formality in Italy

- Use "Lei" (formal you) with professors, officials, shopkeepers you don't know, and
  older people. Switch to "tu" with peers, classmates, and once invited to.
- Greetings: "Buongiorno" (morning/day), "Buonasera" (late afternoon/evening),
  "Ciao" only informally. "Salve" is a safe neutral greeting.
- A light handshake on first meeting; two cheek "kisses" (left then right) among friends.
- Address a professor as "Professore/Professoressa" plus surname until told otherwise.
- "Permesso?" when entering someone's home or squeezing past. "Prego" means
  you're welcome / go ahead / please.
- Italians value a warm, unhurried tone. A little small talk before business is normal.
