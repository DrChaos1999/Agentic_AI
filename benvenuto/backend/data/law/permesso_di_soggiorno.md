# Permesso di soggiorno (residence permit) — basics for students

Non-EU students staying in Italy longer than 90 days must apply for a permesso di
soggiorno per studio within 8 working days of arrival. You submit the application kit
at a Poste Italiane "Sportello Amico" post office, then attend an appointment at the
Questura (police headquarters) to give fingerprints.

While the permit is being processed you are legally present if you hold the postal
receipt (ricevuta) plus your visa. Travel within the Schengen area on the receipt
alone is generally NOT guaranteed and can be refused at borders — carry your visa and
check before booking. Renewals should be started at least 60 days before expiry.

This document is informational only. Always verify current rules with the Questura,
the Prefettura, or your university's international student office before acting.
