# General inspection safety

Stop equipment when a visible defect may release fragments or cause secondary damage. Apply the site's isolation and lockout/tagout procedure before touching powered equipment. A qualified technician must verify every AI recommendation.

# Blowholes and porosity

Blowhole-like cavities can be associated with trapped gas, contamination, inconsistent forming conditions, or upstream material quality. Quarantine the affected batch, examine material cleanliness, review temperature and pressure records, and use a qualified integrity test before release.

# Cracks

Cracks can propagate under fatigue, thermal cycling, excessive clamping, impact, or brittle material conditions. Remove the component from service when propagation could cause failure. Inspect the crack under magnification, check alignment and loading, and review thermal history.

# Breakage

A broken edge or body may indicate overload, handling impact, fixture interference, or crack propagation. Stop the station if debris can enter moving equipment. Inspect adjacent parts, guards, fixtures and handling paths before restart.

# Frayed edges

Frayed edges are often related to abrasion, worn cutting or grinding tools, unstable support, or incorrect feed settings. Inspect tool wear and alignment, remove loose material, and sample nearby products from the same process window.

# Uneven grinding or surface

Uneven surfaces may be caused by grinding-wheel wear, fixture misalignment, spindle instability, or variable feed rate. Measure the profile, inspect tooling, verify fixture geometry, and compare current process parameters with the approved recipe.

# Human approval and work orders

The analysis agent may draft a work order, but it must not create one unless a human explicitly approves the action. The work order should record the image, model version, risk evidence, retrieved examples, recommended actions and approver identity.
